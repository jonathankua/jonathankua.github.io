Biography

I am currently Senior Lecturer in Internet of Things within the School of Information Technology at Deakin University in Melbourne, Australia.

I am an academic researcher in the Networking and Telecommunications & Cyber-Physical Systems lab within the Centre for Internet of Things ECOsystems Research and Experimentation (CITECORE), and I co-lead the “Next-generation IoT communications infrastructure, analytics and intelligence” research program at Deakin.

I received my Ph.D. in Telecommunications Engineering from Swinburne University of Technology, under the primary supervision of Professor Grenville Armitage and A/Prof Philip Branch. My PhD research was industrially funded by Netflix, Inc. Prior to joining Deakin, I was affiliated with the Centre for Advanced Internet Architectures (CAIA) and the Internet For Things Research Group at Swinburne.

Research Interests

I am currently recruiting students and interns for 2024-2025. If you are interested, please email me with your CV, academic transcripts, and a brief statement about yourself. Strong academic background and motivation/interest in our group's research are desirable. I will reply if there is a potential match.

My research interests are in the broad areas of telecommunications and data networking, including:

  • Data transport protocols, TCP congestion control and active queue management

  • Adaptive multimedia streaming and content delivery

  • Low-latency networking for Internet of Things and Industry 4.0

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) for networking
