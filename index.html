Biography

I am currently a Lecturer in Internet of Things within the School of Information Technology at Deakin University in Melbourne, Australia. I am a member of the Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) lab within the Centre for Internet of Things ECOsystems Research and Experimentation (CITECORE), and I co-lead the “Next-generation IoT communications infrastructure, analytics and intelligence” research program at Deakin.

I earned my PhD in Telecommunications Engineering within the School of Software and Electrical Engineering at Swinburne University of Technology, under the primary supervision of Prof Grenville Armitage and A/Prof Philip Branch. My PhD research was industrially funded by Netflix, Inc. I was previously affiliated with the Centre for Advanced Internet Architectures and the Internet For Things Research Group at Swinburne.

During my time at Swinburne, I studied the intricate interactions between Internet-based adaptive streaming systems and bottleneck active queue management (AQM) schemes, and developed a suite of novel client-side techniques that leverages the properties of emerging AQM schemes to optimise the user experience of video streaming applications.

My PhD research was internationally recognised with a second runner-up of the DASH-IF Best PhD Dissertation Award on “Algorithms and Protocols for Adaptive Content Delivery over the Internet” and commended locally within the University with the 2019 Swinburne Outstanding Thesis Award.

Research Interests

My research interests are in the broad areas of telecommunications and data networking, including:

  • Data transport protocols, TCP congestion control and active queue management

  • Adaptive multimedia streaming and content delivery

  • Low-latency networking for Internet of Things and Industry 4.0

  • Artificial Intelligence for networking

I am actively looking for highly motivated students and collaborators to work with me in these research areas.

If you are interested, please feel free to drop me an email.
